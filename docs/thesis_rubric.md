# MSc IS Thesis Evaluation Rubric

## Grading Scale
Poor (<4) | Fail (5) | Sufficient (6) | Good (7) | Very Good (8) | Excellent (9) | Exceptional (10)

---

## Research — 60% weight

### Originality
- **6**: Variation of existing work, applying existing approach to another problem
- **7**: Original elements within a larger existing framework. Publishable at workshop/conference
- **8**: New and original, fresh perspective. Publishable at second-tier conference
- **9**: High originality, thought-provoking. Publishable at top-tier conference
- **10**: Daring and high-risk, will steer the subfield. Top paper at top conference

### Technical Skills
- **6**: Minor modifications to existing tool/model. Superficial or no validation
- **7**: Major modifications based on literature. Basic validation measures
- **8**: Organise data, perform commonly used checks and some advanced analyses
- **9**: Thorough checks, advanced and original analyses
- **10**: Complete control of data, high-level analyses that surprised supervisor and examiner

### Experimental Evaluation
- **6**: Execute an experiment designed by someone else (no critical error assessment)
- **7**: Execute experiment designed by someone else, takes error/uncertainty into account qualitatively
- **8**: Judge existing experiment setup and include modifications. Quantitative error/uncertainty consideration
- **9**: Setup experiment tailored to research questions. Quantitative error/uncertainty. Nearly flawless execution
- **10**: Precisely the right experiments with perfect execution

### Knowledge of Study Domain
- **6**: Understands subject matter at textbook level
- **7**: Understands subject matter and related research; incorporates it for the problem at hand
- **8**: On top of subjects, able to add new knowledge to the study domain
- **9**: Understands relevant knowledge in-depth, can place the field in a new light
- **10**: In-depth understanding, profoundly new insights to the field

### Reflection
- **6**: Minimal reflections on own work
- **7**: Reflects on outcomes with common lessons learned
- **8**: Understands pros/cons and insights for improvement
- **9**: Reflects and learns in a way that helps other researchers in the field
- **10**: Full sync with work and its limitations, resulting in completely new insights

---

## Thesis — 30% weight

### Use of Literature and Theoretical Background
- **6**: Relevant background used, occasional errors, incomplete literature study
- **7**: Relevant background, minimal errors, almost complete literature study
- **8**: Nicely synthesized background tailored to research, complete literature study
- **9**: Clear, complete and relevant background, complete literature study
- **10**: Perfectly tailored background, complete (at points even over-complete) literature study

### Thesis Organisation
- **6**: Correct main structure but some sections not logical, some ambiguity in placement
- **7**: Mostly correct, only certain placements illogical; sections have clear unique functions
- **8**: Correct hierarchy, logical ordering, all information in correct place with few exceptions, appropriate level of detail in most places
- **9**: Well-structured, clear unique sections, logical ordering, appropriate detail throughout
- **10**: Perfect structure and balance, no section too long or short, all sections in harmony

### Writing
- **6**: Decently written with some errors/typos. Method/experiment not always in logical order. Minimal insights
- **7**: Clearly written with minor issues. Appropriate visualisations, standard analyses
- **8**: Well written, few mistakes. Clear method, great visualisations, interesting lessons learned
- **9**: Well written, practically no errors. Detailed, thought-provoking experiments and insights. Visually pleasing with novel analyses
- **10**: Perfectly written, flawlessly structured, great balance and visualisations. Analyses provide new insights to the thesis and subfield

---

## Attitude — 10% weight

### Independence
- **6**: Supervisor sets out tasks; student performs them mostly independently
- **7**: Student selects and plans tasks together with supervisor, performs independently
- **8**: Adopts new skills mostly independently, asks for help when needed
- **9**: Scientific-level knowledge; explores solutions independently, builds skills where necessary
- **10**: No assistance needed, completely aware of the problem, independently written thesis

### Keeping to Schedule
- **6**: Final version overdue without valid reason but research finished as expected
- **7**: Finished within planned period (or overdue with good reason), research as expected
- **8**: Finished within planned period, 5% more research than expected
- **9**: Finished within planned period, 10% more research than expected
- **10**: Finished within planned period, exceptional and in-depth research within timeframe
